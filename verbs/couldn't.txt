Ladies, Start Your Engines  Attempting to simulate the talons of manicured women, male interior designers wore paper clips on their fingers when they designed dashboard buttons for the 1996 Ford Taurus and Cadillac Catera.  According to Life magazine _ the whole of the latest issue is about America's passion for the automobile _ more than half the car-buyers in the U.S. are women. Manufacturers trying to figure out what the heck those ladies want have come up with lumbar support for the pregnant, smooth upholstery designed to be kind to pantyhose and ``crowned'' buttons for those dashboards. Ford designer Nevenka Schumaker says that new cars' ``soft, swoopy'' shapes are supposed to be appealing to females.  Drag racer Shelly Anderson says that the Dodge Neon, Toyota Camry and Mercedes 190 appeal to women; the Porsche 911, Corvette and Lexus 400 are appealing to men.  Meanwhile, the latest version of Gallup's annual survey has found that the only profession rated less favorably than members of Congress is car salesmen.
